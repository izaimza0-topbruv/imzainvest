Junior ISA Guide 2026: Everything Parents Need to Know

As a parent or guardian in the UK, one of the smartest financial moves you can make for your child is opening a Junior ISA. The 2025/26 tax year gives you until 5 April 2026 to make the most of this powerful tax-free savings vehicle. With an annual allowance of ¬£9,000 per child, a Junior ISA can grow to a substantial nest egg by the time your child reaches 18.

As a parent or guardian in the UK, one of the smartest financial moves you can make for your child is opening a Junior ISA. The 2025/26 tax year gives you until 5 April 2026 to make the most of this powerful tax-free savings vehicle. With an annual allowance of ¬£9,000 per child, a Junior ISA can grow to a substantial nest egg by the time your child reaches 18.

But with so many platforms and options available, how do you choose? This guide walks you through everything you need to know about Junior ISAs in 2026.

What Is a Junior ISA?

A Junior ISA (JISA) is a tax-free savings and investment account designed specifically for children under 18. Any growth, interest, or dividends earned within the account are completely tax-free, allowing your child's money to compound without the drag of income tax.

The key difference from a regular savings account: your child cannot touch the money until they turn 18. This makes it perfect for long-term wealth building‚Äîwhether you're saving for university fees, a house deposit, or simply giving them a financial head start.

The 2026 Junior ISA Allowance: ¬£9,000 Per Year

For the 2025/26 tax year (running until 5 April 2026), you can contribute up to ¬£9,000 per child per year to a Junior ISA.

Here's what's important:

  • Separate allowance: This ¬£9,000 is separate from your own ISA allowance‚Äîcontributing to your child's JISA doesn't eat into your ¬£20,000 adult ISA limit.
  • Use it or lose it: Any unused allowance doesn't carry over to the next tax year. If you don't contribute ¬£9,000 by 5 April, you lose that opportunity for that year.
  • Multiple children: Each child has their own ¬£9,000 allowance. If you have three children, you can invest up to ¬£27,000 across their accounts.
  • Anyone can pay in: Parents, grandparents, aunts, uncles‚Äîanyone can contribute to a child's JISA, though only parents/guardians can open it.

Stocks & Shares Junior ISA vs Cash Junior ISA

The first decision you'll make is which type of Junior ISA to open. You can actually have both running simultaneously and split the ¬£9,000 allowance between them.

Stocks & Shares Junior ISA

Best for: Long-term growth, children with years until they turn 18.

A Stocks & Shares Junior ISA invests your money in investment funds, ETFs, bonds, or individual shares. Over a long time horizon (15+ years), stock market investments historically deliver higher returns than savings accounts.

Pros:

  • Potential for significant growth over time
  • Flexibility to choose your own investments
  • Professional fund management available on most platforms
  • Takes advantage of compound growth

Cons:

  • Investment value can fluctuate (falls as well as rises)
  • You could get back less than you put in
  • Requires basic investment knowledge or willingness to learn
  • May feel riskier for cautious parents

Cash Junior ISA

Best for: Lower risk, younger children, parents who want stability.

A Cash Junior ISA works like a savings account‚Äîyour money earns interest, but there's no investment risk. Some platforms offer 3-4% interest rates (though rates vary).

Pros:

  • Capital is protected (no investment risk)
  • Interest is guaranteed (up to the stated rate)
  • Simple to understand
  • Peace of mind for risk-averse parents

Cons:

  • Interest rates may not keep pace with inflation
  • Lower long-term returns than stocks & shares
  • Money tied up until age 18

How to Choose

Open a Stocks & Shares JISA if:

  • Your child is under 14 (more time to recover from market dips)
  • You're comfortable with some volatility
  • You want maximum long-term growth potential

Open a Cash JISA if:

  • Your child is already 15-17 (preservation matters more than growth)
  • You prefer guaranteed, stable returns
  • You want simplicity and no investment decisions

Hybrid approach: Many parents split the allowance‚Äîsay ¬£5,000 into Stocks & Shares and ¬£4,000 into a Cash JISA for balanced exposure.

How to Open a Junior ISA in 5 Minutes

Step 1: Choose your platform

Pick from the options above based on your investment style and budget.

Step 2: Gather documents

  • Your child's full name, date of birth, and National Insurance number
  • Proof of address (utility bill, council tax letter)
  • Your own ID and proof of address

Step 3: Apply online

Visit the platform's website and complete the Junior ISA application. Most take 5-10 minutes.

Step 4: Fund your account

Link your bank account and make your first deposit. Minimum amounts vary (¬£1 to ¬£1,000 depending on platform).

Step 5: Choose your investments

If opening a Stocks & Shares JISA, select your funds or shares. If you're unsure, many platforms offer ready-made portfolios for children (e.g., "Growth Portfolio" or "Balanced Fund").

What Happens When Your Child Turns 18?

When your child reaches 18, their Junior ISA automatically converts into an adult Stocks & Shares ISA in their own name. This is crucial: at 18, the money becomes theirs to control.

Key points:

  • They have access: Unlike the locked Junior ISA, they can now withdraw money or add more (up to their ¬£20,000 adult ISA allowance).
  • Tax-free status continues: Any future growth remains tax-free as long as it's invested through the ISA wrapper.
  • Platform choice: Some platforms (like Hargreaves Lansdown) automatically convert the account; others may require action.
  • Their ISA allowance: At 18, they get their own separate ¬£20,000 annual ISA allowance. The converted Junior ISA continues to be invested separately.

Pro tip: Have a conversation with your child around age 16-17 about what will happen. It's a great teaching moment about investing, compound growth, and financial responsibility.

Tax-Free Status: Why It Matters

Here's why Junior ISAs are powerful:

Example: If you invest ¬£9,000 per year for 12 years (from age 6 to 18), with average 6% annual growth:

  • In a Junior ISA (tax-free): ¬£153,000
  • In a regular savings account (3% interest, taxed): ¬£120,000
  • Difference: ¬£33,000 extra‚Äîall because of tax-free growth

This is the power of the ISA wrapper: tax savings compound alongside your money.

Key Rules and Restrictions

You must know these:

1. One of each type only: Your child can have maximum one Cash JISA and one Stocks & Shares JISA open at any time. They can't have two Trading 212 JISAs, for example.

2. Can't transfer between providers: Unlike adult ISAs, you can't easily transfer a JISA to another platform. Once opened, it stays where it is.

3. Parental access: Parents/guardians can see the balance and receive statements, but the child has legal ownership (they own the money, even though you control it until 18).

4. Child Trust Fund holders: If your child has an old Child Trust Fund (for those born between 2002-2011), you can transfer it to a Junior ISA without using your ¬£9,000 allowance that year.

5. Contributions stop at 18: No more contributions can be made after their 18th birthday. That's why maximising the allowance every year matters.

Common Questions About Junior ISAs

Q1: Can I move my child's Junior ISA to a different platform?

A: Not directly. Unlike adult ISAs, Junior ISAs aren't easily transferable. However, you can:

  • Close the existing JISA (you'll get the cash back)
  • Open a new JISA elsewhere
  • Reinvest the funds

This creates a "break" where you lose the tax-free wrapper temporarily. It's not ideal, so choose your platform carefully.

Q2: Who can contribute to a Junior ISA?

A: Anyone‚Äîparents, grandparents, aunts, uncles, friends, anyone. Only the parent or legal guardian can open it, but anyone can pay in. This makes Junior ISAs great for family contributions.

Q3: What happens if I don't use the full ¬£9,000 allowance?

A: It's lost forever. You can't carry over unused allowance to the next tax year. If you only save ¬£5,000 this year, the remaining ¬£4,000 is gone. This is why it's critical to prioritise contributions before 5 April.

Q4: Can my child access the money before 18?

A: No, not in normal circumstances. The money is locked until age 18. This is actually a feature‚Äîit prevents your child from accessing funds impulsively and ensures long-term growth.

Q5: Are there tax implications if my child earns money from the JISA?

A: No. The entire point of an ISA is to be tax-free. Growth, interest, dividends‚Äîall completely tax-free. Your child won't pay income tax, capital gains tax, or dividend tax on JISA returns.

Final Thoughts

Junior ISAs are one of the best financial tools available to UK parents. The tax-free growth, the locked-in commitment to saving, and the decades of compound growth make them a no-brainer if your child is under 18.

Don't let April 5 pass without making the most of this year's allowance. Even if you can only spare ¬£2,000-3,000, it's worth doing. Over 18 years, that grows to a life-changing sum.
