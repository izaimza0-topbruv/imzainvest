FAQ for first-time UK investors

Do I need to choose individual stocks to use a Stocks and Shares ISA?

No. You can use funds or ETFs only. In fact, many beginners start with broad index funds/ETFs first.

Can I open more than one Stocks and Shares ISA in the same tax year?

Current ISA rules have changed over time, so always check current HMRC/provider guidance. Even when multiple subscriptions are allowed across types/providers, total annual allowance still applies.

How much should I start with?

Whatever is sustainable. Consistent ¬£50-¬£200 monthly can be better than one random ¬£1,000 burst followed by nothing.

Should beginners wait for a market crash?

No one reliably times this. Many people use regular investing to reduce timing stress.
